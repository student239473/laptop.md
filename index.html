About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879â€“1955) was a theoretical physicist, widely recognized as one of the greatest scientific minds in history. Born in Ulm, Germany, Einstein revolutionized the field of physics with his theories of relativity, which reshaped our understanding of time, space, and energy. His most famous equation, ( E = mc^2 ), established the relationship between energy and mass, laying the groundwork for modern nuclear physics.

Einstein's work extended beyond science; he was a staunch advocate for peace and civil rights. After fleeing Nazi Germany in the 1930s, he settled in the United States, where he worked at Princeton University. He continued to contribute to science, philosophy, and politics until his death in 1955.

Timeline of Albert Einstein's Life

  • 1879: Born on March 14 in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1905: Published four groundbreaking papers, including the theory of special relativity.
  • 1921: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Fled to the United States to escape the rise of the Nazis.
  • 1939: Warned President Roosevelt about the potential for nuclear weapons.
  • 1955: Passed away on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey.
